Leatherback Turtle

Population : Leatherback Turtles

Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans.

Hikkaduwa, a seaside village in Sri Lanka, is the habitat of critically endangered Leatherback turtles. These magnificent animals make major contributions to the marine ecosystem and act as markers of environmental health.

The presence of Leatherback turtles in Hikkaduwa has significant ecological significance. Leatherback sea turtles are critical for sustaining marine biodiversity since they are the biggest of all sea turtle species. They are well-known for their unusual diet, which largely consists of jellyfish, which aids in the regulation of jellyfish populations and prevents their uncontrolled expansion. As a result, Leatherback turtles serve an important role in maintaining the marine food web's equilibrium. Leatherback turtles use Hikkaduwa as a nesting location, returning year after year to deposit their eggs. Their nesting activities are facilitated by the sandy beaches. However, these nesting locations are threatened by a variety of factors, including habitat deterioration, coastal development, and human activities. To preserve the survival of Leatherback turtles, these nesting places must be protected and conserved.

Furthermore, public awareness and responsible tourism are critical to the protection of the Leatherback turtle. Educating tourists about the importance of these turtles and supporting environmentally friendly habits will assist to decrease human effect on their nesting locations. Tourists may help by not disrupting nesting habitats, avoiding single-use plastics, and supporting local conservation initiatives. Leatherback turtles in Hikkaduwa are not only magnificent creatures, but they are also critical to the maintenance of marine ecosystems. We can conserve the nesting places of Leatherback turtles and ensure the survival of this endangered species by implementing conservation measures, increasing awareness, and encouraging responsible tourism, so contributing to the general health and variety of our marine ecosystem.
