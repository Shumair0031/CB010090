Hikkaduwa, a Sri Lankan coastal hamlet, is home to a strong population of Olive Ridley turtles. These lovely species are important to the local environment and draw visitors from all over the world. The study investigates the importance of Olive Ridley turtles in Hikkaduwa, highlighting their protection and the necessity for sustainable activities.

The existence of Olive Ridley turtles in Hikkaduwa is extremely important for the environment. These sea turtles are an important element of the marine food chain, eating jellyfish, crabs, and tiny aquatic creatures. They help to maintain a healthy environment by managing the population of certain species. Furthermore, by eating on seagrass beds, Olive Ridley turtles help manage their growth, avoiding overgrowth and ensuring a healthy environment for other marine creatures. Hikkaduwa is an Olive Ridley turtle nesting location, where they come year after year to lay their eggs. TheseÂ nesting events, are beautiful displays that attract both residents and visitors. However, due to rising human activity and habitat damage, these turtles' survival is under risk. However, the existence of these turtles and their nesting locations are under threat owing to rising human activity and habitat loss. It is critical to increase awareness about their vulnerability and the need of habitat conservation.

Olive Ridley turtle conservation activities are undertaken in Hikkaduwa. Local organizations and the government have collaborated to undertake measures including as beach patrols, nest removal, and hatchery initiatives. These programs try to protect the eggs from poachers, predators, and other disruptions, ensuring that they hatch and are released successfully. Furthermore, supporting ethical tourism is critical for the survival of Olive Ridley turtles. Visitors to Hikkaduwa must be taught on the importance of these turtles and urged to conduct environmentally responsible behavior. Olive Ridley turtles at Hikkaduwa symbolize nature's delicate balance and the necessity of conservation. We can safeguard the survival of these gorgeous birds and conserve the fragile ecology in which they live by conserving their nesting locations, increasing awareness, and practicing sustainable tourism.
