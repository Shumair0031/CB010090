Hawksbill Turtle

Population : Hawksbill Turtles

West Atlantic (Caribbean), Indian, Australia, Solomon Islands and Indo-Pacific Oceans

Hikkaduwa, a gorgeous seaside town in Sri Lanka, is lucky to have a robust Hawksbill turtle population. These amazing organisms make substantial contributions to the local environment and are essential for the health of coral reefs.

The existence of Hawksbill turtles in Hikkaduwa is extremely important for the environment. They are not only a biological asset as a critically endangered species, but they also act as indicators of the general health of the marine environment. Hawksbill turtles are sponge feeders, typically eating on sponges found on coral reefs. Their feeding habits aid in the regulation of sponge development, maintaining the longevity and variety of corals, which in turn sustain a wide range of marine animals.Hawksbill turtles play an important part in the dispersal of coral reef seeds, in addition to preserving coral reef health.They eat diverse invertebrates that reside on coral reefs and excrete the undigested seeds as they migrate from one reef to the next, assisting in the formation and extension of coral colonies. This symbiotic interaction between turtles and coral reefs emphasizes the need of turtle protection. Hawksbill turtles use Hikkaduwa as both a breeding and feeding place. Hawksbill turtles, like their Olive Ridley counterparts, suffer a variety of problems, including habitat deterioration, pollution, and unsustainable fishing techniques.

Coral reefs are being destroyed as a result of climate change and human activity, putting their future in danger. It is critical to create awareness about the need of conservation measures in order to conserve these priceless species and their ecosystems. Hawksbill turtle conservation efforts are undertaken at Hikkaduwa. Conservation groups, in collaboration with local communities, are actively engaged in projects like as beach monitoring, nest conservation, and public awareness campaigns.
