Green Turtle

Population : Green Turtles

Raine Island, Great Barrier Reef, Australia

The Hikkaduwa Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a shining example of effective green turtle conservation efforts (Chelonia mydas). The hatchery has become a key sanctuary for the endangered species, playing a critical role in their survival thanks to its determined purpose to conserve and maintain these gorgeous creatures.

The hatchery, located in the lovely seaside town of Hikkaduwa, acts as a beacon of hope for green turtles. This conservation center's major goal is to conserve and nurture turtle eggs until they hatch, protecting the vulnerable baby turtles from natural predators and other hazards. The hatchery's committed staff of professionals and volunteers work diligently to provide a safe environment for these beautiful animals in their early stages of existence. During the nesting season, female green turtles emerge from the ocean to lay their eggs on Hikkaduwa's sandy beaches. Vigilant conservationists at the hatchery painstakingly find and transport the eggs to a safe place within the hatchery, keeping them from falling victim to poachers or environmental risks. This proactive strategy. The hatchlings are gently released back into the water once the eggs hatch. This critical phase not only helps to maintain the natural cycle of these wonderful species, but it also promotes public awareness about the necessity of turtle conservation.

Visitors visiting the hatchery have the rare chance to watch the release, which fosters a stronger connection and knowledge of the need of protecting these beautiful marine species. Aside from conservation activities, the Hikkaduwa Hatchery also participates in educational projects to raise awareness about green turtle conservation. The hatchery hopes to inspire the next generation of environmental stewards through seminars, lectures, and interactive programming.
