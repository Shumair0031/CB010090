Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery was established in 1986 by a local fisherman named Kosgoda Don Chandrasiri.

Hikkaduwa, on Sri Lanka's southern coast, is known for its clean beaches, vivid coral reefs, and abundant marine life. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, which is dedicated to the protection of endangered sea turtle species, is one of the area's most popular attractions. Mr. Chandrasiri was worried about the region's diminishing sea turtle population, which was being slaughtered for their meat, shells, and eggs. He decided to take action and began collecting turtle eggs found on beaches, which he would then hatch in a safe setting before releasing the hatchlings back into the ocean.

The gathering of turtle eggs from beaches is one of the hatchery's main duties. The team meticulously marks the nests and collects the eggs, which are subsequently nurtured in a specially equipped hatchery. The hatchlings are housed in tanks until they are large enough to be released into the ocean. Visitors to the hatchery may observe the process and even help release the hatchlings back into the wild. The hatchery also serves as a refuge for damaged or ill turtles. Before being released back into the water, these turtles are treated by staff and given the required care and attention. Furthermore, the hatchery undertakes research and teaching activities to enhance awareness of the value of sea turtles conservation.

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery has proven effective in boosting the area's sea turtle population. Every year, the hatchery releases thousands of hatchlings, contributing to the broader conservation efforts of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. Furthermore, the hatchery has contributed to raising awareness in the local community about the need of safeguarding sea turtles and their ecosystems. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a shining example of Sri Lanka's effective conservation efforts. The hatchery is a monument to the hard work and commitment of its employees and volunteers, who have worked relentlessly to safeguard endangered sea turtle species. Visitors to the hatchery not only get to see the incredible work being done to safeguard these species, but they also get to interact with them to contribute to the conservation efforts themselves.
