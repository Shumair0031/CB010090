About: Kosgoda Hatchery

To protect the population of turtles that visit the shores to nest

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, located on Sri Lanka's southwestern coast, is an important sanctuary dedicated to the conservation and preservation of sea turtles. This hatchery is critical in protecting fragile turtle populations and creating awareness about the need of turtle conservation. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a haven for several sea turtle species, including Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Green, and Hawksbill. The hatchery exists to safeguard turtle eggs and to provide a secure environment for hatchlings to emerge and start their adventure in the ocean. The gathering and incubation of sea turtle eggs is one of the hatchery's key duties.

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is critical in teaching visitors and local residents about the importance of sea turtle protection. Visitors learn about the life cycle of sea turtles, the challenges they face, and the measures that may be taken to protect them through guided tours and instructive lectures. The hatchery aims to create a more turtle-friendly environment and inspire conservation activities beyond its immediate reach by increasing awareness and fostering responsible tourism.The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a beacon of hope for Sri Lanka's marine turtles. It acts as an example for sustainable practices and a catalyst for good change in conserving these majestic species for future generations through its committed conservation activities and educational programs.
